```
PHASE 1: Validation (Months 1-5)
├─ 50 outbound conversations
├─ 8-12 qualified leads
├─ 3-5 POC candidates

├─ 3 reference customers producing warm leads

├─ 15-30 customers acquired in 12 months
```

- **Tier 1 (Enterprise)**: 60-90 days (high ACV, longer evaluation)
- **Tier 2 (Mid-market)**: 30-45 days (moderate ACV, faster decision)
- **Tier 3 (SMB/Self-serve)**: 14-30 days (low ACV, product-led)

- **Pipeline**: Leads in pipeline at each stage (qualified, POC, proposal, closed)

- **Sales Velocity**: Average days from first conversation to signature

## 7. NEGOTIATION & PRICING STRATEGY

### Pricing Model

**Annual Subscription** (simplest model):

| Tier | Gov Throughput | Max Tenants | Price/Year | Best For |
|------|---|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | 100 req/sec | 10 | $25K | Early-stage SaaS, evaluation |
| **Growth** | 1K req/sec | 100 | $100K | Mid-market SaaS, growing teams |
| **Enterprise** | 10K req/sec | 1000+ | $300K+ | Large CPaaS, fintech, regulated |

**Pricing Drivers**:
- Throughput (requests/sec) = primary driver
- Tenant count = secondary
- Support tier = optional add-on
- Compliance certifications (SOC2, HIPAA, etc.) = premium

### Discounting Strategy

**Tier 1 (First 3 Customers)**:
- Offer: 20% discount on Year 1 (build reference customers)
- Condition: Willing to be reference + provide testimonial
- ACV: $120K (Enterprise), $80K (SaaS), $240K (Infrastructure)

**Tier 2 (Customers 4-10)**:
- Offer: 10% discount Year 1, standard pricing Year 2+
- Condition: 3-year commitment or annual with 10% price lock

**Tier 3 (Customers 11+)**:
- Standard pricing
- Volume discounts for 5+ departments within enterprise

### Negotiation Playbook

**Common Objections & Responses**:

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We need 3-year pricing locked in" | "Happy to lock pricing for Year 1. Year 2-3 will reflect inflation + feature additions. Standard is 2% annual increase." |
| "Your price is 2x competitors" | "Our <50ms latency and cryptographic receipts eliminate $XXX in compliance/manual costs. What's your current cost of manual management?" |
| "We need to build this ourselves" | "Possible, but would take 6-12 months + 1.5-2 FTE engineering time ($300K+). TAI is fully built and battle-tested." |
| "Let us evaluate 3 vendors" | "Fair. We're happy to be evaluated. Here's our benchmark data. When do you need a decision?" |
| "We're over budget for 2026" | "I understand. Is there budget for Q3 2026 planning? If so, we can start with a POC now, decision in Q2." |
| "Price is too high for our stage" | "What's the manual effort cost today? If it's >$50K/year, TAI pays for itself in Year 1." |

**Negotiation Limits**:
- Don't discount below: **$75K/year for Growth, $200K/year for Enterprise** (below this, unit economics don't work)
- Multi-year deals: Lock Year 1 pricing only
- Non-profit/academic: Special program, 50% discount (limited slots)

## 10. EXPANSION PLAYBOOK (12-Month ACV Growth)

### Goal: 3x ACV within 12 months through expansion + upsell

**Baseline (Year 1)**:
- 3 customers × $200K average ACV = $600K ARR
- Target: $1.8M ARR by end of Year 2 (3x ACV per customer cohort)

### Expansion Tactics

**Tactic 1: Departmental Land & Expand (Q2-Q4 Year 1)**
- Identify: Where else in customer organization could TAI help?
- Example: If deployed in Product Ops, could also help Finance (billing) or Revenue Ops (commissions)
- Approach: CSM partners with customer executive sponsor to identify adjacent teams
- Result: +20-30% ACV per customer by Q4 Year 1

**Tactic 2: Tier Upgrade (Reactive, as customers scale)**
- Monitor: Throughput metrics, approaching limits
- Alert: When customer reaches 70% of tier limit
- Offer: Upgrade to next tier, lock in pricing for Year 2
- Result: +50-100% ACV per customer (if scaling rapidly)

**Tactic 3: Feature Upsell (Q3-Q4 Year 1)**
- New features TAI releases: Advanced analytics, custom receipt formats, compliance certifications
- Approach: CSM presents feature to customer, discusses fit with their roadmap
- Pricing: $20-50K per feature/year
- Result: +$15-25K ACV per customer with features

**Tactic 4: Multi-Year Commitment (Renewal conversation)**
- Timing: 90 days before contract ends (e.g., Month 9 of Year 1)
- Offer: 3-year commitment at locked pricing (2% annual increase), 15% discount Year 1 only
- Benefit: Removes renewal risk, improves CAC payback
- Result: +10% Year 1 deal value (from discount), locked revenue stream

### Expansion Revenue Waterfall (Year 1 → Year 2)

```
Year 1 Revenue: $600K (3 customers × $200K avg ACV)
                ↓
+ Departmental expansion (20% of 3 customers): +$120K
+ Tier upgrades (if applicable): +$0-75K (depends on customer scaling)
+ Feature upsells: +$45K (3 × $15K)
+ Multi-year commitment deals: +$60K (3 × 10% deal value locked)
+ New customer cohort (7 customers × $150K avg ACV): +$1,050K
                ↓
Year 2 Revenue Target: $1.8M+ ARR
```

**Q1-Q2 Year 1** (Deployment phase):
- Focus: On-time delivery, production stability
- Expansion: Monitor for obvious adjacent use cases

**Q3 Year 1** (Optimization phase):
- Formal: Executive business review presenting ROI data

**Q4 Year 1** (Expansion phase):

## 11. RETENTION & RENEWALS

### Retention Metrics & Targets

| Metric | Year 1 Target | Year 2 Target | Year 3 Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gross Retention Rate (GRR)** | 90% | 95% | 95%+ |
| **Net Retention Rate (NRR)** | 120% | 130% | 140%+ |
| **Churn Rate** | <10%/year | <5%/year | <3%/year |
| **Expansion Rate** | 20% | 25% | 30% |

**Definitions**:
- **GRR**: % of customers renewing (excluding expansion)
- **NRR**: GRR + expansion revenue (shows expansion value)
- **Churn**: Customers not renewing / Total customers
- **Expansion Rate**: % growth in ACV from existing customers

### Renewal Playbook (Timeline)

**Month 9 of contract** (3 months before expiration):
- CSM: Schedule renewal conversation
- CSM: Review Year 1 ROI data, gather customer testimonials
- Focus: Multi-year commitment (3 years at locked pricing)

**Month 10 of contract** (2 months before expiration):
- CSM: Executive business review (CFO/VP + TAI leadership)
- Present: Year 1 ROI metrics, Year 2 roadmap, expansion opportunities
- Propose: 3-year renewal deal with 10% discount Year 1 only

**Month 11 of contract** (1 month before expiration):
- Sales: If not renewed, follow up with discount offer
- Focus: Understand objections, address concerns
- Fallback: 1-year renewal at standard pricing

**Month 12 of contract** (at expiration):
- If renewed: Celebrate, set Year 2 success plan, plan expansions
- If not renewed: Conduct win/loss interview, understand why, stay in touch

### Win/Loss Interview Template

**For Customers Who Don't Renew** (exit interview):

```
1. Primary reason for not renewing?
   - Not delivering promised ROI
   - Built alternative in-house
   - Switching to competitor
   - Budget cuts, deprioritized
   - Performance/reliability issues
   - Other

2. What could TAI have done differently?
   - Better customer success
   - Different feature set
   - Lower pricing
   - Better support/documentation
   - Other

3. Would you reconsider in future?
   - No chance
   - Possible if [X improves]
   - Yes, later this year
   - Yes, next year

4. Can we stay in touch?
   - For future opportunities
   - As vendor for other needs
   - General Slack/email updates
```

### Preventing Churn (Indicators & Actions)

**Early Warning Signs** (CSM should catch):
- Reduced usage (entitlement check volume declining)
- Missed business reviews (less engagement)
- Feature requests stop coming (losing interest)
- Late payments or budget questioning
- Customer leadership changes (new buyer)

**CSM Actions to Prevent Churn**:
1. Proactive check-in if usage drops >20%
2. Escalate to manager if customer goes silent for 30+ days
3. Schedule executive business review to reconnect
4. Offer expansion opportunity to increase perceived value
5. Provide case study / referral opportunity (gives them win to justify TAI)
