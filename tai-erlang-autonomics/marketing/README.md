## Brand Voice Quick Rules

### DO:
- Lead with impact ("Save $1.2M annually")
- Use concrete numbers (47 seconds, not "fast")
- Be active voice ("Systems make decisions")
- Assume technical intelligence
- Use market metaphors
- State facts confidently

### DON'T:
- Qualify weakly ("may," "might," "could")
- Use marketing jargon ("leverage," "synergy")
- Be cute (technical audience values clarity)
- Oversimplify (audience understands Kubernetes)
- Use superlatives without proof
- Mix audiences (write for engineers in all materials)

## Critical Success Factors

1. **Messaging Clarity**: We don't say "AI for infrastructure." We say "Value-indexed systems measure business impact and decide autonomously."

2. **Proof Points**: We don't argue. We show. (89%, $1.2M, 47s repeated everywhere.)

3. **Inevitable Framing**: We position as "By 2030..." not "We help you..." Inevitability creates urgency.

4. **Market Metaphor**: We use economic language (prices, efficiency, equilibrium) that resonates with technical audiences.

5. **First-Mover Advantage**: We claim the category early. By the time incumbents notice, we're the category standard.

## FAQ

**Q: How much should we budget for launch marketing?**
A: $50K for paid media (LinkedIn, Reddit, newsletters) + organic/earned media (press, content, community). Most impact comes from free channels (press, content, word-of-mouth) + sales outreach.

**Q: Who should own the launch execution?**
A: Dedicated launch lead (likely VP Marketing) + core team (Product, Sales, Community). See LAUNCH_CHECKLIST for role breakdown.

**Q: What if we don't hit the targets?**
A: Most important: momentum compounds. Week 1 total matters more than Day 1. Continue Week 2 execution, adjust messaging based on what's resonating. Pivoting is normal.

**Q: When should we start writing about this externally?**
A: Press release Day 0, blog posts Days 0-7, then ongoing. But: all messaging must use the framework in MESSAGING.md to ensure consistency.

**Q: How do we know if the messaging is working?**
A: Track demo request conversion rate, pilot signup rate, customer conversation quality. If CTOs are repeating your message back to you, it's working.
