## Key Themes Across All Documents

### Theme 1: Blue Ocean Strategy

We're not competing with Okta, Datadog, Splunk on their terms (better features, lower price). We're creating a new market (outcome-indexed pricing) where old competitors are irrelevant.

- **Okta** invented "per-user IAM pricing" → we invented "compliance audit pass pricing"
- **Datadog** invented "per-GB observability pricing" → we invented "incident detection accuracy pricing"
- **Splunk** invented "per-GB compliance pricing" → we invented "audit pass outcome pricing"

Each of them dominates their ocean. We're creating a new ocean entirely.

### Theme 2: Incumbent Business Model Lock-In

Incumbents are trapped. Their business model rewards consumption (more users, more data, more incidents). Ours rewards efficiency (fewer incidents, faster deployments, better compliance). They cannot change without destroying their business model.

- **Okta** benefits when you hire more people → we benefit when you reduce incidents
- **Datadog** benefits when you collect more data → we benefit when you detect incidents faster
- **Splunk** benefits when you generate more logs → we benefit when you pass audits

This is not a fair fight. They're incentivized to make you less efficient; we're incentivized to make you more efficient.

### Theme 3: Regulatory Tailwinds

Healthcare CMS is mandating value-based care pricing by 2028. SEC/FINRA are pushing outcome-based compliance. This creates $50-70B of customer demand for "prove ROI to regulators." Incumbents cannot meet this demand; we can.

- Healthcare will have no choice (CMS mandate)
- Finance will have no choice (SEC/FINRA requirement)
- Enterprise will follow (board demands ROI transparency)

### Theme 4: Customer Success = Company Success

Our business model aligns incentives. We succeed when you succeed. This is the opposite of every incumbent.

- Customer wants to reduce incidents → we want to reduce incidents (aligned)
- Customer wants compliance audit passes → we want compliance passes (aligned)
- Customer wants to improve deployment velocity → we want faster deployments (aligned)

Every decision we make should strengthen this alignment.

### Theme 5: Moat is Non-Technology

Our defensibility does NOT come from patents, architecture, or technology. It comes from:
1. **Regulatory approval** (hard to change once established)
2. **Customer outcome history** (24 months of data proving ROI)
3. **Switching cost** (customers won't move because they lose historical proof)

This is stronger than tech moat because it's harder to copy, and gets stronger with time.

## Red Flags (Things That Could Prove Us Wrong)

1. **Regulatory pushback** - If healthcare/finance regulators reject outcome measurement as "too risky," entire thesis collapses
2. **Incumbent fast response** - If Okta/Datadog pivot aggressively to outcome-indexed within 12 months, we lose first-mover advantage
3. **Customer acquisition difficulty** - If it takes 18+ months to close first customer instead of 6 months, we'll run out of capital
4. **Outcome measurement too complex** - If customers say "we can't define our outcomes," adoption stalls
5. **Market adoption slower than forecast** - If market shift happens 2031-2033 instead of 2026-2029, we run out of runway

**Our risk mitigation**:
- Get Big Four approval early (reduces regulatory risk)
- Lock in customers with 2-year contracts (reduces churn risk)
- Raise 24-month runway (reduces capital risk)
- Build pre-built outcome templates (reduces complexity risk)
- Aggressive go-to-market execution (reduces adoption risk)
