## Sensitivity Analysis

### Revenue Sensitivity (ACV Impact)

```
If average ACV decreases by 10% ($41K → $37K):
  Year 1 impact: -$113K
  Customer count needed: +3 (30 → 33 customers)
  Still positive economics (just more customer-focused)

If average ACV increases by 10% ($41K → $45K):
  Year 1 impact: +$113K
  Higher-margin customers possible (enterprise segment)
  Conservative to assume ACV decrease (more customers, lower ACV)
```

### Sales Team Productivity Sensitivity

```
If new sales rep closes 20 customers/year (vs. 24 forecast):
  Year 1 impact: -$180K revenue
  Still achieves $950K ARR (not bad, just slower ramp)

If new sales rep closes 28 customers/year (vs. 24 forecast):
  Year 1 impact: +$180K revenue
  Achieves $1.3M ARR (upside case)

Scenario: Hiring high-performing reps can dramatically change outcome
Strategy: Hire top 10% reps, invest in sales training
```

### Customer Churn Sensitivity

```
Current churn: 0% (all customers happy)
Conservative assumption: 2% monthly churn (industry standard)

Impact on Year 1:
  If 2% monthly churn kicks in Month 6:
  Lost revenue: ~$50-75K

Mitigation: Focus on customer success (CSM hire, weekly check-ins)
```
