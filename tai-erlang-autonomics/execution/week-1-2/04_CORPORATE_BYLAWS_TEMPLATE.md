## ARTICLE I: MEETINGS OF SHAREHOLDERS

### Section 1.1: Annual Meeting

The annual meeting of the shareholders of TAI Autonomics, Inc. (the "Corporation") shall be held on the last Friday of April in each year, at 10:00 AM, unless such day is a legal holiday, in which case the meeting shall be held on the next succeeding business day, at such place, on such date and at such time as shall be specified in the notice of the meeting, for the purpose of electing the directors of the Corporation and for the transaction of such other business as may come before the meeting.

### Section 1.2: Special Meetings

Special meetings of the shareholders may be called at any time by the President, the Board of Directors, or the shareholders holding not less than one-tenth (1/10) of all shares entitled to vote at the meeting.

### Section 1.3: Notice of Meetings

Written notice stating the place, day, and hour of the meeting and, in case of a special meeting, the purpose or purposes for which the meeting is called, shall be delivered not less than ten (10) nor more than sixty (60) days before the date of the meeting.

### Section 1.4: Quorum

The holders of a majority of the shares entitled to vote at any meeting, present in person or represented by proxy, shall constitute a quorum at such meeting. If a quorum is not present, the meeting may be adjourned by a majority of the shares present.

### Section 1.5: Voting Rights

Each outstanding share, regardless of class, shall be entitled to one (1) vote upon each matter submitted to a vote at a meeting of shareholders.

### Section 1.6: Action Without Meeting

Any action required to be taken at any meeting of the shareholders, or any action which may be taken at any meeting of the shareholders, may be taken without a meeting if the action is taken in accordance with all applicable provisions of the Delaware General Corporation Law (DGCL).

## ARTICLE II: BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 2.1: General Powers

The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by or under the direction of its Board of Directors, which may exercise all such powers of the Corporation and do all such lawful acts and things as are not by statute, by the Certificate of Incorporation, or by these Bylaws directed or required to be exercised or done by the shareholders.

### Section 2.2: Number and Term

The number of directors shall be fixed from time to time by the Board of Directors, but shall be not less than one (1) nor more than nine (9). The directors shall be elected at the annual meeting of shareholders and shall hold office until their successors are duly elected and qualified.

**Initial Board Structure**:
- Directors: 1-3 (expandable by board action)
- Term: One year (until successor elected)
- Removal: Directors may be removed with or without cause by shareholder vote or board action per DGCL

### Section 2.3: Qualifications

Directors need not be shareholders of the Corporation or residents of the State of Delaware.

### Section 2.4: Election of Directors

Directors shall be elected by a plurality of the votes cast by the shares entitled to vote in the election at a meeting at which a quorum is present. Cumulative voting is not permitted.

### Section 2.5: Board Meetings

- **Regular Meetings**: The Board shall hold at least quarterly meetings (Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct)
- **Special Meetings**: May be called by the President or any director
- **Notice**: No notice required for regular meetings; special meetings require 24 hours' notice
- **Quorum**: A majority of directors constitutes a quorum
- **Voting**: Each director has one vote; majority of quorum decides

### Section 2.6: Duties and Liabilities

The directors shall be responsible for:
- Oversight of corporate operations and strategy
- Financial reporting and audit oversight
- Compliance with laws and regulations
- Risk management
- Shareholder value creation

**Limitation of Liability**: To the fullest extent permitted by Delaware law, no director shall be personally liable to the Corporation or its shareholders for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty.

**Indemnification**: The Corporation shall indemnify any director or officer against reasonable expenses (including attorneys' fees) incurred in defending any claim or proceeding by reason of being a director or officer, provided such person acted in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Corporation.

## ARTICLE III: OFFICERS

### Section 3.1: Officers

The Board of Directors shall elect or appoint the following officers:
- **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**: Day-to-day management
- **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)**: Financial oversight (or Treasurer)
- **Secretary**: Records and shareholder communications

### Section 3.2: CEO Powers and Duties

The CEO shall:
- Be the principal executive officer
- Preside at board meetings and shareholder meetings
- Recommend policies and strategies
- Execute contracts and agreements
- Represent the Corporation in business dealings

### Section 3.3: CFO/Treasurer Powers and Duties

The CFO shall:
- Manage corporate funds and investments
- Deposit funds with authorized depositaries
- Pay bills and approve expenditures
- Maintain financial records
- Provide quarterly financial reports to board

### Section 3.4: Secretary Powers and Duties

The Secretary shall:
- Keep minutes of shareholder and board meetings
- Send notices and communications
- Maintain corporate records and stock register
- Authenticate corporate documents
- Maintain the corporate seal

### Section 3.5: Officer Compensation

Officer compensation shall be fixed by the Board of Directors and may include:
- Base salary
- Performance bonuses
- Equity compensation (stock options, RSUs)
- Benefits (health insurance, 401k, etc.)

## ARTICLE IV: STOCK

### Section 4.1: Authorized Stock

The Corporation is authorized to issue:
- **Common Stock**: 10,000,000 shares, par value $0.0001 per share
- **Preferred Stock**: 5,000,000 shares, par value $0.0001 per share (undesignated)

The Board of Directors may establish different classes and series of Preferred Stock with such rights, preferences, and privileges as determined by the Board.

### Section 4.2: Issuance of Stock

Stock may be issued for consideration as fixed by the Board, which may consist of:
- Cash
- Services rendered
- Property
- Other securities or obligations of the Corporation

### Section 4.3: Stock Certificates

- Shares may be certificated or uncertificated
- If certificated, certificates shall be signed by the CEO and Secretary
- Certificates shall contain:
  - Name of corporation
  - Statement that shares are registered
  - Number and class of shares
  - Par value
  - Rights, preferences, and limitations

### Section 4.4: Transfer of Stock

- Shares may be transferred by delivery of the certificate or by book-entry
- The Corporation may treat the registered owner as the owner of shares
- The Corporation may require reasonable evidence of proper transfer

### Section 4.5: Restricted Stock & Vesting

**Initial Founder Grants**: Subject to 83(b) elections and vesting schedules:
- **Founder A**: [Shares] with [Vesting Schedule]
- **Founder B**: [Shares] with [Vesting Schedule]
- Standard: 4-year vest with 1-year cliff

**Repurchase Rights**: Corporation retains right to repurchase unvested or forfeited shares at par value or as specified in restricted stock agreements.

### Section 4.6: Lost, Stolen, or Destroyed Certificates

Upon satisfactory proof of loss, theft, or destruction of a certificate, and on payment of a reasonable indemnity, the Corporation shall issue a new certificate in its place.

## ARTICLE V: SHAREHOLDER AGREEMENTS

### Section 5.1: Voting Agreements

Shareholders may enter into agreements regarding:
- Voting of shares
- Election of directors
- Board matters
- Transfer restrictions

### Section 5.2: Right of First Refusal

The Corporation shall have the right to purchase any shares offered for sale by a shareholder at the same price and terms as the proposed third-party sale.

### Section 5.3: Co-Sale Rights

If a shareholder with preferred stock co-invests alongside venture capital, co-sale rights may be granted allowing investors to participate in founder share sales.

---

## ARTICLE VI: DIVIDENDS

### Section 6.1: Declaration of Dividends

The Board of Directors may declare and pay dividends upon the outstanding shares of the Corporation in such manner and upon such terms as it shall determine, subject to restrictions in the Certificate of Incorporation and Delaware law.

**Initial Policy**: TAI Autonomics shall not declare dividends during the initial growth phase. Profits will be reinvested in operations.

### Section 6.2: Dividend Restrictions

No dividend shall be declared or paid if:
- It would render the Corporation insolvent
- It would impair the capital of the Corporation beyond permitted levels under DGCL

---

## ARTICLE VII: INDEMNIFICATION

### Section 7.1: Mandatory Indemnification

The Corporation shall indemnify any director, officer, employee, or agent:
- If such person acted in good faith
- If the person reasonably believed their conduct to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Corporation
- In any derivative or third-party suit, except for claims initiated by the Corporation

### Section 7.2: Expense Advancement

The Corporation may advance reasonable expenses (including attorneys' fees) to any indemnified party pending the outcome of any claim.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: CORPORATE RECORDS

### Section 8.1: Required Records

The Corporation shall maintain:
- Minutes of all shareholder and board meetings
- Stock register showing shareholders and share ownership
- Financial statements
- Employee records (if applicable)
- Contract archives

### Section 8.2: Record Access

Shareholders have the right to inspect corporate records during normal business hours upon written request, provided such request is not for improper purposes.

### Section 8.3: Destruction Policy

Corporate records shall be retained for a minimum of:
- **Tax Records**: 7 years
- **Board/Shareholder Minutes**: Indefinitely
- **Financial Records**: 7 years
- **Contracts**: Duration + 7 years
- **Personnel Records**: Duration + 3 years

---

## ARTICLE IX: FISCAL MATTERS

### Section 9.1: Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the Corporation shall be the calendar year (January 1 - December 31) unless changed by the Board of Directors.

### Section 9.2: Corporate Seal

The Corporation may have a corporate seal with the name of the Corporation engraved thereon. Use of the seal is optional.

### Section 9.3: Bank Account

The CFO/Treasurer shall maintain all corporate funds in one or more bank accounts in the Corporation's name, separate from personal accounts.

### Section 9.4: Loans to Officers

The Corporation shall not make loans to any officer or director except as permitted under Delaware law and only upon approval by the Board of Directors.

---

## ARTICLE X: AMENDMENTS

### Section 10.1: Amendment Authority

These Bylaws may be altered, amended, or repealed by:
- **Board Action**: Majority vote of the Board of Directors, or
- **Shareholder Action**: Majority vote of outstanding shares

### Section 10.2: Notice of Amendment

Any amendment shall be adopted with notice to all directors or shareholders.

---

## ARTICLE XI: CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

### Section 11.1: Disclosure Required

Any director or officer having a material interest in any transaction or contract with the Corporation shall:
- Disclose the interest to the Board before action is taken
- Abstain from voting on such transaction
- Submit full details of the transaction in writing

### Section 11.2: Permitted Transactions

A transaction involving a director or officer shall not be voided if:
- The interested party made full disclosure
- The remaining board members approved the transaction in good faith
- The transaction was fair to the Corporation

---

## ARTICLE XII: DISSOLUTION AND LIQUIDATION

### Section 12.1: Voluntary Dissolution

The Corporation may be dissolved with the approval of the Board and shareholders as required by Delaware law.

### Section 12.2: Distribution of Assets

Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed:
- First: To satisfy debts and liabilities
- Next: To satisfy preferred stock liquidation preferences (if any)
- Finally: Pro-rata to common shareholders
