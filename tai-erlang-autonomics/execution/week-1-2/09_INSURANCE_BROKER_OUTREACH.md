## Executive Summary

TAI Autonomics requires three primary insurance policies in Year 1:

1. **Directors & Officers Liability (D&O)**: Protects founders/board from legal claims
2. **General Liability**: Standard business coverage (errors, accidents)
3. **Cyber Liability**: Covers data breaches, privacy violations
4. **Professional Liability** (E&O): Covers defects in software/services

**Task**: Contact 3-5 startup-focused insurance brokers for competitive quotes and select coverage.

---

## Insurance Types & Coverage

### 1. Directors & Officers Liability (D&O) - CRITICAL

**Covers**: Legal defense costs for founders/officers/directors in lawsuits alleging:
- Breach of fiduciary duty
- Wrongful termination claims
- Securities violations
- Employment disputes
- Shareholder lawsuits

**Cost Range**: $3,000-$8,000/year for early-stage startups
**Coverage Amount**: $1M-$3M typical
**Deductible**: $25K-$50K typical

**Why Needed**:
- Investors will require before funding
- Protects personal assets of founders
- Required for D&O indemnification in bylaws to be valid
- Legal claims are expensive to defend (defense costs alone: $100K+)

**Recommended Carrier**: Admitted carriers specializing in startups
- Beazley
- Travelers
- AIG
- Chubb (high-end)

---

### 2. General Liability Insurance

**Covers**: Bodily injury, property damage, legal defense
- Person injured at office
- Damage to third-party property
- Basic legal defense costs

**Cost Range**: $500-$2,000/year
**Coverage Amount**: $1M/$2M typical
**Often Bundled**: With D&O in startup package

---

### 3. Cyber Liability Insurance

**Covers**:
- Data breach costs (notification, credit monitoring)
- Privacy lawsuits (customer data disclosure)
- Ransomware/malware costs
- Business interruption due to cyber attack
- Forensics and recovery costs

**Cost Range**: $2,000-$5,000/year for software companies
**Coverage Amount**: $1M-$2M typical
**Critical For**: AI/data-processing companies like TAI Autonomics

**Recommended Carriers**:
- Zurich
- Allianz
- AIG
- Beazley

---

### 4. Professional Liability / Errors & Omissions (E&O)

**Covers**:
- Defects in software or services provided
- Customer claims of poor performance
- Data loss or system failures caused by TAI Autonomics
- Contractual liability (if specified in customer agreements)

**Cost Range**: $2,000-$5,000/year
**Coverage Amount**: $1M-$2M typical
**Critical For**: Software/AI companies

**Note**: This is often called "Technology E&O" or "Software E&O"

### Phase 3: Finalist Selection (Day 10-14)

**Task**: Narrow to 2-3 finalists and request clarifications

**Key Questions to Ask**:

1. **D&O Specific**:
   - Are defense costs inside or outside the limit?
   - Is there a severability clause (separate coverage per insured)?
   - What's the retroactive date?
   - Any exclusions for securities claims?

2. **Cyber Specific**:
   - Does coverage include ransomware/malware?
   - What's the maximum for breach notification costs?
   - Is incident response (forensics) included?
   - Is regulatory defense covered?

3. **E&O Specific**:
   - Does "contractual liability" include customer contracts?
   - What's the definition of "defect" or "error"?
   - Is there a warranty exclusion or limitation?

4. **General**:
   - Any startup-specific discounts?
   - What's the renewal rate lock guarantee?
   - Are there any co-insurance requirements?
   - Can we get a policy summary in writing?

---

### Phase 4: Selection & Binding (Day 14-21)

**Task**: Select preferred broker and bind coverage

**Selection Criteria**:

Optimal choice = Lowest cost + Adequate coverage + Responsive broker

**Preferred Winner Profile**:
- Total annual premium: $6,000-$10,000
- D&O coverage: $2M
- Cyber coverage: $1M+ with forensics included
- E&O coverage: $1M+ with contractual liability
- Broker: Dedicated startup support
- Startup discount: 15%+ available

**Next Steps After Selection**:

1. **Notify Broker**: Accept quote and request to proceed
2. **Provide Info**: Broker may request additional information
3. **Underwriting**: Broker submits application to carriers (3-5 business days)
4. **Policy Issuance**: Receive draft policy for review (3-5 business days)
5. **Review & Bind**: Founder reviews policy, approves terms, broker binds coverage
6. **Payment**: Pay annual premium (typically due at binding or within 30 days)
7. **Certificate**: Request certificate of insurance for records

**Timeline to Coverage**: 10-15 business days from application
