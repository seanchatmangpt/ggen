## RESOLUTION 4: FOUNDER RESTRICTED STOCK GRANTS

**WHEREAS**, the Board recognizes the contributions of the founders to the development and incorporation of the Corporation;

**WHEREAS**, the Board deems it appropriate and in the best interests of the Corporation to issue shares of Common Stock to the founders as compensation for their services and dedication;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, that the Board of Directors hereby authorizes and approves the issuance of the following restricted shares of Common Stock to the founders, subject to the terms and conditions of the Restricted Stock Agreements:

### Grant Details

| Recipient | Class | Shares | Price | Total Cost | Vesting Schedule | Strike Price |
|-----------|-------|--------|-------|------------|------------------|--------------|
| [Founder A Name] | Common | 1,000,000 | $0.0001 | $100.00 | 4 years with 1-year cliff | $0.0001 |
| [Founder B Name] | Common | 1,000,000 | $0.0001 | $100.00 | 4 years with 1-year cliff | $0.0001 |

### Terms and Conditions

1. **Restricted Stock Agreements**: All shares shall be issued pursuant to Restricted Stock Agreements in the form presented to the Board and approved hereby. The agreements shall be executed by the recipients and the Corporation.

2. **Vesting Schedule**:
   - 4-year vesting period with 1-year cliff
   - Cliff: 25% of shares vest at 12-month anniversary
   - Monthly vesting thereafter: 1/48th of total shares per month
   - Upon vesting, shares become fully owned; upon departure, unvested shares forfeited

3. **Forfeiture**:
   - If founder terminates employment or services before vesting, all unvested shares shall be forfeited to the Corporation
   - Forfeited shares return to authorized but unissued shares

4. **Repurchase Rights**:
   - Corporation retains right to repurchase unvested or forfeited shares at par value ($0.0001)
   - Right applies during vesting period and for 90 days after departure

5. **Transfer Restrictions**:
   - Shares subject to right of first refusal in Corporation
   - Shares may not be transferred without Board approval
   - All transfers subject to Restricted Stock Agreement terms

6. **Voting & Dividends**:
   - Founder may vote all shares (vested and unvested)
   - Founder entitled to dividends on all shares (pro-rata)

7. **Section 83(b) Elections**:
   - Founders may file 83(b) elections with IRS within 30 days of share receipt
   - Corporation cooperates and maintains copies in corporate records
   - Founders responsible for tax consequences and CPA coordination

## RESOLUTION 5: APPROVAL OF 2026 STOCK OPTION PLAN

**WHEREAS**, the Board recognizes the need to attract, retain, and motivate employees and service providers of the Corporation;

**WHEREAS**, the Board believes that a stock option plan would serve these objectives and align employee interests with those of the Corporation and shareholders;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, that the Board of Directors hereby:

### Plan Approval

1. **Adopts** the **TAI Autonomics, Inc. 2026 Stock Option Plan** (the "Plan"), substantially in the form presented to the Board

2. **Reserves** a total of 2,000,000 shares of Common Stock for issuance under the Plan

3. **Authorizes** the Administrator (Board or Compensation Committee) to grant options under the Plan to eligible participants

### Plan Parameters

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Option Pool | 2,000,000 shares | ~20% of issued and outstanding at inception |
| Pool % of Fully Diluted | 20% | Industry standard for startups |
| Exercise Price | FMV on grant date | Fair market value determination required |
| Vesting Schedule | 4 years with 1-year cliff | Standard startup vesting |
| Exercise Period | 10 years from grant | ISO and NSO both permitted |
| Eligible Participants | Employees, directors, consultants, advisors | Board discretion on grants |

### Delegation of Authority

1. **Grant Authority**:
   - CEO authorized to grant options up to 50,000 shares per individual grant
   - Grants exceeding 50,000 shares require Board approval
   - All grants subject to Plan terms and Board oversight

2. **Grant Documentation**:
   - Each option grant evidenced by Stock Option Agreement (standard form approved by Board)
   - Grant specifies: recipient, shares, exercise price, vesting schedule, termination conditions

3. **Plan Administration**:
   - Administrator: Board of Directors (or Compensation Committee if established)
   - Administrator has discretion to interpret Plan and make determinations
   - Plan terms supersede any conflicting individual grant agreements

### Key Terms

1. **Vesting**: Options vest over 4 years with 1-year cliff (customizable per grant)
   - If termination: unvested options forfeited immediately
   - If vesting complete: option exercisable for 90 days post-termination

2. **Tax Treatment**:
   - Plan permits both Incentive Stock Options (ISOs) and Non-Qualified Stock Options (NSOs)
   - ISO treatment available for employees only; NSOs available for all
   - Exercise price must equal or exceed FMV on grant date (per tax code)

3. **Exercise**:
   - Option exercisable by written notice + payment of exercise price
   - Payment methods: cash, net exercise, broker-assisted, or other Board-approved methods
   - Exercise period: 10 years from grant date (or earlier upon termination)

4. **Share Dilution**:
   - Plan shares included in fully diluted capitalization for investor purposes
   - Reserved shares reduce availability for future capital raises
   - Board shall monitor dilution and report to shareholders annually

### Shareholder Approval

- If required by law or exchange rules, Plan shall be submitted for shareholder approval
- Plan contingent on shareholder approval if legally required

**WHEREAS**, Delaware law permits corporations to indemnify directors and officers for actions taken in good faith;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, that:

1. **Indemnification**: The Corporation shall indemnify any director, officer, employee, or agent:
   - If such person acted in good faith
   - If the person reasonably believed their conduct to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Corporation
   - In any derivative or third-party suit (except suits initiated by Corporation itself)

2. **Defense Costs**: The Corporation shall advance reasonable expenses (including attorneys' fees) pending outcome of claims

3. **Insurance**: The Board authorizes the Corporation to obtain Directors & Officers liability insurance to protect directors and officers

4. **Individual Agreements**: The CEO is authorized to execute individual indemnification agreements with directors and officers in standard form

5. **Limitations**: Indemnification shall not apply to:
   - Breach of duty of care where director acted with gross negligence or recklessness
   - Breach of duty of loyalty or dishonesty
   - Transactions requiring disclosure where disclosure was not made
   - Prohibited dividend payments

## RESOLUTION 12: CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY

**WHEREAS**, the Board recognizes the importance of preventing conflicts of interest and maintaining ethical standards;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED**, that the Board adopts the following Conflict of Interest Policy:

1. **Disclosure**: Directors, officers, and employees shall disclose any material interest in proposed transactions with the Corporation

2. **Abstention**: Interested parties shall abstain from voting and discussions regarding the transaction

4. **Violations**: Violations may result in removal from office or other appropriate remedies
