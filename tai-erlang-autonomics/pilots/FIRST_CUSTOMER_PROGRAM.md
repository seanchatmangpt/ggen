## Part 2: Value Hypothesis (What We'll Measure)

### The TAI Value Equation for Customer #1

We don't sell "inventory management." We sell **margin recovery through automated coordination**.

```
TAI Value =
  (Margin Recovery from Overstock Prevention) +
  (Margin Improvement from Price Optimization) +
  (Operational Savings from Automation) +
  (Working Capital Freed from Better Turns) -
  (TAI Fees)
```

### Baseline Metrics (Pre-TAI)

We must establish these **before go-live** to prove impact.

**Inventory Metrics:**

| Metric | Typical Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **Inventory Accuracy** | 88-94% | If inaccurate, coordination breaks |
| **Overstock Rate** | 8-12% of inventory | Dead inventory costs margin |
| **Overstock Aging** | 40-60 days average | Longer aging = deeper discounts |
| **Stockout Rate** | 3-5% of orders | Direct revenue loss |
| **Turns Per Year** | 3-5 for apparel | Slow turns = working capital drag |
| **Inventory Carrying Cost** | 20-30% of value/year | Rent, labor, shrink, obsolescence |

**Margin Metrics:**

| Metric | Typical Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **Overstock Markdown Depth** | 30-50% discount | TAI should reduce this |
| **Out-of-Stock Revenue Loss** | 2-4% of GMV | TAI should reduce this |
| **Excess Inventory Write-off** | 1-3% of inventory value | TAI should reduce this |
| **Gross Margin (blended)** | 55-65% | TAI improves by 2-5 points |

**Operational Metrics:**

| Metric | Typical Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **Hours/Week on Inventory Coordination** | 10-20 hours | TAI should reduce this 60-80% |
| **Average Cycle Time (order to fulfillment)** | 2-3 days | TAI enables faster processing |
| **Forecast Accuracy (next 30 days)** | 70-80% | TAI should improve to 85-90% |

**Financial Metrics:**

| Metric | Typical Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **Working Capital in Inventory** | 30-45 days of COGS | TAI frees up cash |
| **Annual Carrying Cost** | $250K-$1.2M | Labor, rent, shrink, insurance |

### Success Metrics (Post-TAI, 90-Day Target)

We measure success not on "adoption" but on **margin recovery**.

**Primary Success Metrics (Must Have All 3):**

1. **Inventory Accuracy** → 96%+
   - Target: +3-5 percentage points improvement
   - Proof: Cycle count vs. system reconciliation
   - Measured: Weekly

2. **Overstock Markdown Reduction** → 35% discount depth (vs. 45% baseline)
   - Target: +10 percentage points margin recovery on markdown items
   - Proof: Price elasticity analysis (same volume, higher price)
   - Impact: $50K-$150K annual margin recovery

3. **Stockout Prevention** → 1-2% out-of-stock rate (vs. 3-5% baseline)
   - Target: -50% reduction in stockouts
   - Proof: Order fulfillment rate by channel
   - Impact: $75K-$200K annual revenue recovery

**Secondary Metrics (Should Have 2+ of 3):**

4. **Operational Efficiency** → 8-12 hours/week freed (vs. 15-20 hours baseline)
   - Target: 40-60% reduction in manual coordination time
   - Proof: Time tracking + task logs
   - Operational value: 1 FTE equivalent

5. **Forecast Accuracy** → 85-90% (vs. 70-75% baseline)
   - Target: +15 percentage points improvement
   - Proof: Weekly actual vs. forecast variance
   - Enables: Smaller buffer stock = freed working capital

6. **Working Capital Improvement** → 10% reduction in inventory value
   - Target: $50K-$200K freed for growth capital
   - Proof: Inventory balance sheet reduction, turns improvement
   - Financial impact: Reinvestment in marketing

### The Proof Formula

We declare "success" when:
```
(Margin Recovery + Operational Savings) / TAI Cost ≥ 2.0x

Example:
- Margin Recovery: $100K (overstock + stockout improvement)
- Operational Savings: $40K (labor freed)
- Total Value Created: $140K
- TAI Cost: $60K/year
- Ratio: 2.33x (SUCCESS)
```

## Part 4: POC Duration & Design

### Why 90 Days? (Not 30, Not 180)

| Duration | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|----------|------|------|---------|
| **30 Days** | Fast, low friction | Insufficient data, no seasonal signal | Too short |
| **60 Days** | 2 complete cycles | Misses weekly/monthly patterns | Borderline |
| **90 Days** | 3 complete cycles, seasonal signal, clear trend | Longer sales cycle, but better proof | BEST CHOICE |
| **180 Days** | Maximum proof | Risk of churn, long opportunity cost | Too risky |

**90 Days = Three Complete Forecasting Cycles**

For most DTC retailers:
- Week 1-2: Baseline + system familiarization
- Week 3-5: TAI learns demand patterns, first optimizations
- Week 6-8: Full benefit realization, clear margin impact
- Week 9-12: Sustained improvement, confidence for renewal

### POC Structure: Managed Pilot (Not DIY)

We don't hand off the customer and hope. We manage this actively.

**TAI's Commitment:**

| Phase | Week | Activity | TAI Resources |
|-------|------|----------|----------------|
| **Setup & Baseline** | 1-3 | Data audit, baseline measurement, integration | 1 customer success + 1 engineer |
| **Launch & Monitoring** | 4-6 | Go-live, daily monitoring, manual optimization | 1 customer success (full-time) |
| **Optimization** | 7-9 | Pricing tweaks, forecast refinement, channel balancing | 1 customer success + 0.5 data scientist |
| **Measurement & Handoff** | 10-12 | Final measurement, case study, expansion planning | 0.5 customer success |

**Customer's Commitment:**

- Weekly sync calls (30 min) to review metrics
- Access to inventory systems + transaction data
- Designate single point of contact (ops lead)
- Willingness to implement TAI recommendations
- Feedback on UX/feature requests

### Go-Live Decision: Gradual vs. Cutover

**Gradual Launch (Recommended):**

- Week 1-2: TAI manages 1 high-turnover SKU category (20% of inventory)
- Week 3-4: Expand to 50% of SKUs
- Week 5+: Full inventory under TAI management

**Advantage:** De-risk, clear learning, customer confidence builds

**Cutover Launch (Alternative):**

- Day 1: TAI manages all inventory simultaneously
- Safety: TAI in "advisor" mode first 2 weeks (human override), then "full control"

**Recommendation:** Gradual for apparel (high SKU count), Cutover for simple portfolios

## Part 6: Discount Strategy (Revenue vs. Proof Points)

### The Psychological Framing

We don't "discount" Customer #1. We **invite them to participate in value creation**.

> "We're building a new category of autonomic supply chain software. You're Customer #1. In exchange for helping us prove the model, we're offering favorable terms that reflect the risk you're taking as an early adopter. But this isn't a discount—it's participation in something new."

### Pricing Structure

**Base Case (Full Price):** $900-1,200/year for $5-50M GMV company

**Customer #1 Terms (Proposed):**

```
Year 1 (POC): $45K
Year 2 (Scale): $120K (+1% of overstock margin improvement)
Year 3+: Standard enterprise rate

OR

Fixed 3-Year Deal: $240K total
- Year 1: $60K
- Year 2: $90K
- Year 3: $90K
```

**Discount Level:** 50-60% off full price (from ~$150K/year to $45-60K/year Year 1)

**Rationale:**

- **Year 1 (POC):** Customer takes on risk (new vendor, unproven). We subsidize.
- **Year 2 (Proof):** As value becomes clear, price increases. Customer locks in wins.
- **Year 3+:** Standard enterprise pricing applies, but customer now a reference.

**Financial Impact:**

```
Full Price Path (if we wait for traction):
- Year 1 Price: $150K × 15 customers = $2.25M
- But: No reference case to sell others
- Outcome: Series A fundraising much harder

Customer #1 Discount Path:
- Year 1 Revenue: $45K (investment in proof)
- Year 2 Revenue: $120K (locked-in 3-year customer)
- Year 3 Revenue: $150K (standard terms apply)
- Total 3-Year: $315K

Multiplier Effect (Because of reference case):
- Enables Customer #2: $150K/year (no discount)
- Enables Customer #3: $150K/year (whale deal, larger)
- Series A Valuation Boost: +$10-15M (from "proof of concept" → "validated model")

Net Present Value: The "discount" pays for itself 3x over.
```

| Red Flag | Severity | Contingency |
