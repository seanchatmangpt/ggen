
# 26. FOREIGN KEY RELATIONSHIP *


*The relationship is a first-class citizen of the domain model.*


---

## Context

JPA relationships (@OneToMany, @ManyToOne) require careful mapping to avoid N+1 queries and Cartesian products. The wrong fetch type (EAGER vs LAZY), wrong cascade setting, or missing mappedBy causes silent correctness bugs that only manifest under load. These bugs are hard to detect in unit tests and expensive to diagnose in production.\n\nOWL object properties are the ideal representation for domain relationships: they have a domain, a range, and cardinality constraints. The OWL cardinality (owl:maxCardinality 1 for many-to-one, no cardinality for one-to-many) maps directly to the JPA relationship type. ggen derives the correct JPA annotations from the OWL cardinality, eliminating the entire class of mapping errors.

## The Problem

**Incorrectly mapped relationships cause performance disasters or data integrity violations.**


The forces at play create tension between competing needs. Without resolution, these forces lead to suboptimal outcomes—complexity where simplicity is needed, fragility where robustness is required, opacity where clarity is essential.


---

## Therefore

Express relationships in the ontology as OWL object properties. ggen generates the correct JPA relationship mapping with appropriate fetch strategies, cascade settings, and join columns. @ManyToOne relationships default to LAZY fetch. @OneToMany relationships use mappedBy pointing to the owning side. @JoinColumn names are derived from the property name. No N+1 queries by default.\n\nFor bidirectional relationships, the ontology declares owl:inverseOf between the two object properties. ggen generates the mappedBy and helper methods (addItem, removeItem) that maintain both sides of the association.

## Implementation in ggen sync


ggen reads owl:ObjectProperty declarations with rdfs:domain and rdfs:range pointing to java26:Entity or java26:AggregateRoot classes. The owl:maxCardinality value determines @ManyToOne (1) vs @OneToMany (unbounded). owl:inverseOf generates the mappedBy parameter and the bidirectional helper methods.
