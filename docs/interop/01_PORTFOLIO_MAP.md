# Portfolio Map: Genesis Distributed Architecture

This document inventories the entire project portfolio and classifies each project by its role in the Genesis-bearing distributed architecture. 

**Core Doctrine:**
- **Genesis** is the pure Chatman Equation foundation.
- **ggen** is the foundry and membrane that manufactures, packages, adapts, validates, and projects Genesis-bearing parts.
- **AtomVM** gives parts custody.
- **WASM** gives parts portability.
- **Rust** gives physical discipline.
- **Genesis** gives the part lawful construction.
- **Receipts** make parts composable.
- **Truex** is the lifecycle for receipted execution trust.
- **wasm4pm/pictl** consume and validate process-evidence projections.
- **open-ontologies** is a public-vocabulary survivability checkpoint.
- **External engines** (QLever, DuckDB, SHACL tools) are validators/consumers, not Genesis replacements.

## Conclusion and Recommendations

### Core Components
- **Genesis Kernel** (`genesis-core-v2`, `genesis-types-v2`, etc.) and the underlying Rust substrate are the absolute core. They are responsible for the pure Chatman Equation construction. 
- *Recommendation:* Cleanse Genesis. Remove all internal JSON representations and enforce that `Pair2` acts strictly as a tuple within a predicate-fixed `RelationPage`.

### Membranes
- **ggen** and **Truex** operate as the membranes and gateways. `ggen` is the manufacturing foundry that projects outputs to the outside world, and `Truex` is the gateway that authorizes and logs consequence. 
- *Recommendation:* Ensure neither dictates core physical truth; they must rely entirely on Genesis receipts.

### Runtime Bodies
- **AtomVM** and **WASM** (`genesis-wasm-shell`) provide the physical encapsulation and portability for interchangeable parts. 
- *Recommendation:* Finalize the `genesis-wasm-shell` so that generated parts can be reliably executed inside an AtomVM sandbox with full receipt emission.

### Validators
- **wasm4pm**, **pictl**, and external engines (**QLever**, **DuckDB**, etc.) act as pure downstream validators and consumers.
- *Recommendation:* Strict isolation. Do not leak validation rules or query engines back into the Genesis Kernel.

### Vertical Proof Surfaces
- **open-ontologies**, **OSTAR**, and specific industry tests (**Telco**, **Doctor**) prove the system survives contact with public reality and complex workflows.
- *Recommendation:* Implement non-cheating, end-to-end tests that trace a boundary from OSTAR input, through ggen manufacturing, into Truex promotion, and out to `open-ontologies` projection.

### Archival Action Items
- Repositories and tools related to **dspygen**, **dslmodel**, **uvmgr**, **ZOE LA**, **UniverseOS**, **BusinessOS**, and **Gitvan** should be **archived or merged**. They represent legacy paradigms that distract from the pure Genesis/Truex architecture.
